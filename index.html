Production Systems Perspective

Why production experience changes how I evaluate systems work

A major part of my technical perspective comes from working on large-scale production systems where small design choices can have large operational consequences. In these environments, system quality is not measured only by benchmark performance, but also by reliability, debuggability, cost efficiency, latency, rollout safety, and resilience under failure.

This experience shapes how I evaluate systems work. I look for clear problem framing, realistic assumptions, careful comparisons, meaningful ablations, and evidence that the proposed approach can generalize beyond a controlled experimental setting.

Research Agenda

Studying how intelligent systems fail when the environment stops being ideal

My current research agenda focuses on reliability and evaluation for production-scale AI and distributed systems. I am interested in how systems behave when information is delayed, retrieval is noisy, context is incomplete, infrastructure is unreliable, or compute budgets are constrained.

  • When does retrieval help, hurt, or waste compute?
  • How do LLM systems behave when critical information arrives late?
  • When do summarization systems introduce temporally incorrect claims?
  • How can distributed graph methods support root-cause analysis in cloud-native systems?
  • How should we evaluate AI systems under realistic production constraints?

Selected Systems

Selected systems and evaluation efforts

LatencyBench

An evaluation framework for studying how LLM systems behave when information arrives late, partially, or after an initial answer has already begun to form. The goal is to measure premature commitment, recovery behavior, and robustness under delayed information availability.

Retrieval Value Maps

A framework for analyzing when retrieval-augmented generation helps, hurts, or wastes compute. The work focuses on understanding retrieval value across query difficulty, answer quality, latency, and cost.

Swarm RCA for Microservices

A production-facing root-cause analysis effort for cloud-native microservices using distributed graph methods. The work focuses on reliability, observability, and diagnosing complex failures across interacting services.

Temporal Hallucination Analysis

An evaluation effort focused on detecting when LLM-generated summaries introduce events that are temporally unsupported, premature, or not grounded in the source content.

These projects study failure modes that are easy to miss in clean benchmarks but expensive in production.
